## Extracted knowledge

The presentation proposes a centralized, course-oriented way for students to manage growing collections of digital academic material. It describes these capabilities:

- uploading common academic documents such as lecture material, assignments, and reference files;
- grouping resources by course or subject;
- previewing PDF and Word documents;
- searching the collection by keywords;
- generating course folders automatically;
- validating uploads and returning useful errors;
- allowing files to be updated or removed;
- operating locally without routine server calls;
- keeping stored study files on the learner's device.

The stated motivation is that unmanaged digital files create confusion, duplication, retrieval delays, and avoidable mental overhead. The proposed design emphasizes consistent categorization, fast local interaction, and a simple browser-based architecture.

## Evidence boundaries

This source supports claims about the described Study Ledger proposal and its intended feature set. It does not establish measured learning gains, prove that local storage is sufficient security, or support Mastery Ledger's evidence-verification, exam-generation, or spaced-review features. Those are separate Mastery Ledger design decisions.
